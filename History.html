Team History

The Minnesota Vikings were established in 1961 in Minneapolis, Minnesota. The team was granted an NFL franchise as part of the league's expansion efforts. The Vikings' name was chosen to pay homage to the Viking heritage prevalent in the region due to its large population of Scandinavian immigrants. The team's iconic purple and gold color scheme was also inspired by this cultural connection.

No team in NFL history has had a more spectacular debut than the Minnesota Vikings did on September 17, 1961. Rookie quarterback Fran Tarkenton came off the bench and threw 4 touchdown passes and ran for 1 to whoop the Chicago Bears 37-13. 8 years later, Fran Tarkenton would lead the Vikings and the “Purple People Eaters” defense to the franchise's first-ever Super Bowl appearance in 1970. Although they fell short in that Super Bowl, the Vikings remained a competitive force in the league for decades, with multiple playoff appearances and division titles. They would also appear in 3 more Super Bowls during the 70s in 74, 75, and 77. Throughout their history, the Vikings have consistently fielded top-tier talent, including Hall of Famers like Randy Moss, Fran Tarkenton, Chris Carter, and Adrian Peterson. The team continues to be a beloved and enduring presence in the NFL, with a dedicated fan base known for their passionate support.
